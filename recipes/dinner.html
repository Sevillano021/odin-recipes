Sicilian-Style Swordfish

Sicilian-style swordfish is an easy and quick recipe, typical of Italian cuisine, especially from Sicily. The fish is served with a Mediterranean sauce made with tomato, olives, capers, and aromatic herbs, giving it lots of flavor and freshness.

Ingredients

Serves 2
  • 2 swordfish steaks
  • 2 ripe tomatoes
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/2 onion
  • 30 g black olives
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • Dried oregano to taste
  • Fresh parsley
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper
  • A splash of white wine (optional)
Difficulty: Easy
  • Total time: 25 minutes
  • Preparation: 5 minutes
  • Cooking: 20 minutes

How to Make Sicilian-Style Swordfish

  1. Peel and chop the onion and garlic. Wash and dice the tomatoes.
  2. In a pan with a little olive oil, saut√© the onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add the tomatoes, sliced black olives, and capers. Cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add oregano, salt, pepper, and, if desired, a splash of white wine. Cook 5 minutes more.
  5. Add the swordfish steaks, cover, and cook for 5 minutes on each side, until the fish is cooked through but still juicy.
  6. Serve the Sicilian-style swordfish sprinkled with chopped fresh parsley and accompanied by the sauce.
